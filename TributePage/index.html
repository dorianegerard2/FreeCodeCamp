Tribute to Marie Curie

Between the discovery of radium in 1898, the Nobel Prize for physics in 1903 and the Nobel Prize for chemistry in 1911, Marie Curie is still considered one of the greatest scientists in history.

Marie Curie in her laboratory, where she carried out all her work on nuclear energy.

Here's a time line of Marie Curie life:

  • 1867 -Maria Sklodowska is born in Warsaw
  • 1891 -Maria Sklodowska moves to Paris
  • 1895 -Maria Sklodowska marries Pierre Curie.She now bears the name of Marie Curie.
  • 1898 -Pierre and Marie Curie discover radium.In front of the audience at the Academy of Sciences, Pierre and Marie Curie announce that they have succeeded in extracting a radioactive element from a mineral: radium.
  • 1903 -Pierre and Marie Curie receive the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1906 -Marie Curie becomes a professor at the Sorbonne At 39, the physicist becomes the first female professor at the Sorbonne. She will teach while continuing her research.
  • 1911 -She receives the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the isolation of radium Marie Curie announces that she has succeeded in isolating metallic radium in its pure state. She was able to determine its atomic mass.
  • 1914 -Marie Curie creates the first mobile radiological service During the First World War, Marie Curie wanted to put her work on radioactivity at the service of the war wounded. This is why it organizes the first mobile radiology service. Assisted by her daughter Irène, she fitted out 18 cars nicknamed "little Curies".
  • 1914 -Foundation of the Radium Institute Created to reconcile scientific advances in radioactivity and medicine, the Institute is made up of two sections: the Pasteur pavilion, directed by Claudius Regaud and the Curie laboratory, directed by Marie Curie. After the war, CLaudius Regaud concentrated his activities on the fight against cancer by radiation. The Radium Institute merged with the Curie Foundation in 1970, giving birth to the Curie Institute.
  • 1934 -Marie Curie dies of leukemia Caused by too long and too intense exposure to radiation, leukemia carried off Marie Curie, then 67 years old. She leaves the torch to her eldest daughter, Irène. Later, accompanied by her husband Frédéric Joliot, Irène Joliot-Curie would follow in the footsteps of her parents by discovering artificial radioactivity.
  • 1995 -Marie Curie becomes the first woman to enter the Pantheon on her own merits. Recognized as one of the greatest scholars of her time, now the most famous scientist in the world, she leaves behind an invaluable scientific and moral legacy.
